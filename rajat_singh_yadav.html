I am a development economist and research consultant specialising in financial inclusion, gender economics, and digital public infrastructure. My work spans the full research-to-policy pipeline — from designing consumer research studies and managing multi-country field surveys to producing high-impact policy briefs for multilateral organisations.

My Ph.D. thesis, "Spatial Financial Inclusion and Credit Interventions for Inclusive Growth" (Gokhale Institute of Politics and Economics, 2024), uses district-level spatial econometrics to map financial exclusion hotspots across India and identify structural drivers of credit access gaps.

Currently, I work as a Research Consultant at Women's World Banking, leading cross-country research on women's digital financial inclusion across India, Vietnam, Indonesia, Cambodia, Laos, Nigeria, and Kenya. I previously consulted for the World Bank, the Asian Development Bank Institute, and the Indian School of Business, where I held a Principal Investigator role on a USD 0.5 million climate-resilience grant.

Research areas

My research is organised around three intersecting themes: spatial and digital financial inclusion — using GIS, Moran's I, and spatial lag/error models to map banking access gaps across Indian districts; gender and household economics — examining women's labour force participation, digital product adoption barriers, and principal-agent dynamics in fintech; and development policy evaluation — applying RCT-informed designs, theory of change frameworks, and mixed-methods approaches to assess programmes in nutrition, livelihoods, and rural governance across South Asia and Sub-Saharan Africa.
